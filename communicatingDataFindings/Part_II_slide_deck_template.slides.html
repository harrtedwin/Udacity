Dataset¶

  • Loan Data from Prosper: This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others
  • The source data (Prosper Loan Data) was from Udacity website.

Since the dataset contains a large volume of columns, I extracted 21 columns that seemed useful for my analysis.

Primary justification for loan applications¶

We can see from the graph above that the majority of borrowers seek loans to pay off their current debt or consolidate new debt. This proportion is significantly higher than individuals who apply for loans for common causes including home remodeling, business, car-related expenses, and medical needs. The most money is typically borrowed for debt consolidation.

Aside from financing their businesses and house related expenses, people appear to rely on loans to pay for their weddings, pay taxes, using the loans to fund for vaccation, child adaption, making large purchases, and student use.

Do borrowers take on more debt than they can reasonably pay off?¶

Right-skewed distribution can be seen in the debt-to-income ratio. Most borrowers appear to choose taking out loans that are affordable. This is understandable because the majority of numbers are spread out between 0 and 1.

The main monetary ranges of existing clients¶

According to the plot, the majority of people make between 50,000 dollars and 74,999 dollars per year, followed by people making between 25,000 dollars and 49,999 dollars. Additionally, a decreased count is seen in all income brackets with greater incomes.

Distribution of Loan Status¶

About 57% of the information is made up primarily of Current loans. Another significant component of the listings, at 34%, is made up of Completed loans. Even though there aren't many loans that default, roughly 7% of them are charged off, suggesting losses for investors. It will be difficult to determine at this time if borrowers have been making their loan payments because the majority of the listings are current loans.

Which occupation gets highest loan amount on an average?¶

According to the bivariate analysis between occupation and loan amount, it can be observed that Invenstor are the ones with highest average loan amount of above 16,000 Dollars followed by Dentist and Pharmasict.

How does loan amount differ with respect to the income range?¶

As per the plot, income range of 124,999 has maximum outliers. Also, for the highest income range starting from 50,000, the loan amount range is also high. However, these ranges do not seem to have any unusual observation/outliers.
